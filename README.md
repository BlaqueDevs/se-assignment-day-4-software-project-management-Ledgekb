## 3. Compare and contrast Agile and Waterfall methodologies. What are the main advantages and disadvantages of each?
Agile:

Overview: An iterative and incremental approach that promotes flexibility and customer collaboration.
Advantages:
Flexibility to adapt to changes.
Continuous feedback from stakeholders.
Faster delivery of functional software.
Enhanced team collaboration and communication.
Disadvantages:
Can lead to scope creep due to constant changes.
Requires active stakeholder involvement, which may not always be available.
Less predictability in terms of timelines and costs.
Waterfall:

Overview: A linear and sequential approach where each phase must be completed before the next begins.
Advantages:
Clear structure and defined phases.
Easier to manage and predict timelines and costs.
Comprehensive documentation at each stage.
Disadvantages:
Inflexibility to changes once the project is underway.
Late discovery of issues, as testing occurs after development.
Risk of delivering a final product that does not meet user needs due to limited feedback during development.

## 6. How does defining the project scope contribute to successful project planning? What is a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), and why is it useful?
Defining the project scope is crucial for successful project planning because it clearly outlines what the project will deliver and what is outside of its boundaries. This ensures that everyone involved has a shared understanding of the goals, deliverables, and constraints, helping to avoid scope creep (uncontrolled expansion of project scope). It also establishes a solid foundation for allocating resources, managing timelines, and setting realistic expectations.

A Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) is a hierarchical breakdown of the project into smaller, manageable components or tasks. Each level represents more detailed aspects of the work required to complete the project. The WBS is useful because it:

Helps organize tasks and assign responsibilities.
Improves cost estimation, scheduling, and resource allocation.
Makes tracking progress easier by breaking complex projects into smaller, more manageable pieces.
Enhances communication by giving everyone a clear picture of the work needed.
## 7. What are the benefits of developing a detailed project schedule, and how can Gantt charts assist in this process?
Developing a detailed project schedule provides several benefits:

Improved Time Management: It outlines when tasks need to be started and completed, helping ensure deadlines are met.
Resource Allocation: It ensures resources (people, equipment, etc.) are assigned efficiently to avoid bottlenecks.
Tracking Progress: It helps track task completion and the overall project’s progress, allowing for timely adjustments if needed.
Enhanced Coordination: A clear schedule improves communication among team members, keeping everyone aligned on timelines.
Risk Management: It allows for the identification of potential delays or conflicts, so contingency plans can be prepared.
Gantt charts assist in this process by visually representing the project schedule. They show tasks on a timeline, making it easy to see the start and end dates, task durations, and dependencies between tasks. This visual tool simplifies the tracking of progress and helps teams identify when critical tasks need to be completed, reducing the risk of delays
